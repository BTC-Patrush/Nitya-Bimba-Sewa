Highlights from the Kathmandu STEM Expo.

March 01, 2026 • Kathmandu, Nepal • 5 min read

The recent Kathmandu STEM Expo brought together over 500 students, educators, and scientific innovators to showcase the power of practical, hands-on learning. Hosted in partnership with the local municipal government, the event marked a significant milestone in our ongoing mission to democratize science education across Bagmati Province.

Igniting Curiosity in Young Minds

For many students in attendance, this was their first opportunity to interact with professional-grade scientific equipment. Our fleet of mobile micro-labs, typically deployed in rural schools, was brought to the center stage. Students engaged directly with experiments covering fluid dynamics, cellular biology, and basic robotics.

The enthusiasm was palpable. Rather than reading about chemical reactions in aging textbooks, students were able to safely mix compounds, observe the results, and formulate their own hypotheses under the guidance of our Vigya Council volunteers.

"Seeing the spark of realization in a student's eyes when they finally understand a complex physical concept through hands-on interaction is exactly why we do this work. It bridges the gap between theory and reality."
— Dr. Ramesh K., Lead STEM Facilitator

Key Highlights from the Expo

The two-day event was packed with workshops, competitions, and collaborative sessions. Some of the most impactful moments included:

  • The Interactive Biology Station: Students utilized high-powered digital microscopes to analyze local water samples, directly connecting their textbook knowledge to local environmental issues.
  • Robotics for Rural Solutions: A showcase where high school students programmed simple robotics designed to automate agricultural tasks, demonstrating incredible problem-solving skills.
  • Teacher Training Workshop: Over 40 local educators participated in our specialized module on how to integrate micro-lab kits into standard municipal curriculums without disrupting the academic calendar.

Students interacting with the NBS mobile micro-labs at the Kathmandu Expo.

Looking Forward: Scaling the Impact

The success of the Kathmandu STEM Expo has solidified our data-driven hypothesis: when provided with the right tools, the potential of rural youth is limitless. Following the expo, Nitya Bimba Sewa has secured commitments to expand our mobile lab fleet by five additional units by the end of 2026.

We extend our deepest gratitude to the volunteers, corporate sponsors, and local authorities who made this event a resounding success. Together, we are not just teaching science; we are building a more capable, innovative future.
