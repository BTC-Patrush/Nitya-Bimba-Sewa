General Information

What exactly does Nitya Bimba Sewa do?

We are an NGO that bridges the gap between scientific research and grassroots social work. We operate across 4 pillars: Science & Education (mobile STEM labs), Environmental Resilience (biodiversity mapping), Community Wellbeing (health & mental health camps), and Leadership & Justice (digital literacy for women and youth).

Is Nitya Bimba Sewa a legally registered organization?

Yes. We are officially registered with the Government of Nepal (Bagmati Province) and affiliated with the national Social Welfare Council (SWC). You can view our legal standing on our Transparency page.

Where are your projects located?

Our headquarters is in Kathmandu, but our fieldwork focuses primarily on rural and marginalized communities within Bagmati Province and neighboring districts (like Makwanpur, Nuwakot, and Chitwan). We are gradually scaling our mobile labs and health camps nationwide.

Donations & Funding

How much of my donation goes directly to the field?

We are proud to state that over 82% of all funding directly supports program execution and fieldwork. The remaining 18% covers necessary administrative, operational, and fundraising costs to keep the organization sustainable. Our accounts are audited annually by an independent CPA.

Are my donations tax-deductible?

For donors within Nepal, donations may be eligible for tax exemption under current national tax laws regarding registered SWC-affiliated NGOs. We provide an official receipt for all donations. International donors should consult their local tax advisor to see if foreign NGO donations qualify for deductions in their country.

Can I donate using eSewa, Khalti, or Fonepay?

Yes! Local giving is crucial to our mission. You can easily make transfers using your digital wallet to our official ID, or by scanning the Fonepay QR code available on our Donate page.

Volunteering & Internships

Do I need a science or medical background to volunteer?

Not at all! While we do need doctors, nurses, and science teachers for specific roles, we heavily rely on general volunteers for logistics, community mobilization, data entry, and digital literacy training. Passion and dedication are the most important requirements.

Will I receive a certificate for my internship?

Yes. All university students who successfully complete our structured 3-to-6-month academic internship program will receive an official experience certificate and a letter of recommendation detailing their specific contributions to our research or fieldwork.

Partnerships & Corporate CSR

How can our company execute our CSR funds through NBS?

We offer turnkey solutions for Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR). We can align your corporate goals with our ongoing projects (like adopting a school lab or sponsoring a health camp). We handle all logistics, government compliance, and provide you with a comprehensive post-project impact report and media assets. Contact our team via the Contact page to request our Partnership Deck.

What is the Vigya Council, and how do I join?

The 'Vigya' (Expert) Council is a network of subject-matter experts (PhDs, doctors, researchers, environmentalists) who volunteer their knowledge to guide our methodologies and peer-review our publications. If you have advanced experience in our core fields and wish to contribute your intellectual capital, you can apply via the Membership section.
